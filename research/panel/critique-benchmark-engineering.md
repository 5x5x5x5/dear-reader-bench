FATAL FLAWS:
- ARCHITECTURAL SELF-CONTRADICTION: the single five-dimension judge call (the claimed 5x cost cut, load-bearing for the $350 figure) is incompatible with the split-visibility design. Humor and voice must NOT see the English source; names, culture, and fidelity MUST see it. One call cannot both include and exclude the source text. Minimum viable design is two calls per (pair, segment, order, judge) — a blind call and a source-aware call — which doubles judging cost (~$700/run now, ~$2.4k at the 45-segment corpus) and invalidates the halo-audit design as written. The proposal's cost model and its core judging architecture cannot both exist.
- THE UNTETHERED-GENERATION HOLE: as specified, no instrument measures correspondence to the source text. Humor and voice are source-blind; NAMES asks only for consistency/grandiosity/phonotactics (not derivation from the source's names); Callback Integrity is pure self-consistency of the system's own renderings; FIDELITY checks the scene description, not the English. A 'translation' system that discards the source and free-writes scene-conditioned bombastic comedy in the target language wins every dimension and both novel metrics. The benchmark as specified measures scene-conditioned creative writing, not translation. The distractor suite has no probe for this (a D-untethered distractor — generate from scene only, never shown the English — would expose it immediately), and its absence is disqualifying for a translation benchmark.
- CROSS-CELL BT AGGREGATION IS STATISTICALLY UNSOUND: DRB = SUM(w_d * theta_d) combines logits from separately-fit Davidson models. BT logit scale in each (language x dimension) cell is set by judge decisiveness/noise in that cell — a dimension where judges agree strongly produces larger-magnitude logits, so effective weights are driven by judge confidence, not the nominal w_d. Macro-averaging across languages additionally inherits the anchor system's per-language competence (theta=0 means 'as good as pinned-Qwen is in THAT language', a different quality level per cell). Anchoring fixes the origin, never the scale; the cross-release comparability claim is false on its face, and doubly false once the proposal's own judge-rotation and corpus-season policies change the instrument between releases. Needs per-cell standardization or a joint hierarchical model — neither is proposed.
- THE PERFORMABILITY GATE IS QUANTITATIVELY BROKEN: (a) the 15% forfeit margin sits inside eSpeak-NG's plausible duration error band — robotic prosody, no comedic pacing/pauses, and notoriously weak Japanese (kanji reading) support mean per-segment duration error of +/-20-30% in exactly the most interesting languages, so forfeits get assigned by TTS quirk at a knife-edge discontinuity; (b) the stated calibration procedure — synthesize the ENGLISH source and confirm ~1.0 — is incoherent: it calibrates eSpeak's English rate and says nothing about its Japanese or Korean rate quirks; per-language calibration requires target-language reference audio the project doesn't have; (c) forfeit-as-deterministic-loss inside the BT likelihood re-mixes compliance with quality — the exact construct-mixing the proposal condemns in v1's 15% weight, relocated into the likelihood where it's harder to see — and creates theta -> -infinity degeneracies for full-forfeit systems (no regularization/prior is specified) and silently corrupts every score if the anchor system itself ever forfeits a segment (nothing guarantees anchor eligibility); (d) the gate is one-sided: no dead-air penalty, so the equilibrium under known judge verbosity bias is every system padding to 1.14x — 'length bias solved physically' is oversold.
- NO HUMAN SIGNAL ANYWHERE IN THE VALIDITY CHAIN: the proposal misreads its own central citation. LitEval's finding is misaligned PREFERENCE (judges prefer machine output over professional human literary translation) — a taste failure that persists in pairwise head-to-head; anchored pairwise (per JP-TL-Bench) fixes scale instability, not taste. Distractors validate gross sensitivity (can the judge spot deliberately flattened prose), never top-of-table discrimination among frontier systems, which is what a leaderboard sells. A shared aesthetic prior across all LLM judge families — the plausible RLHF-flattened preference for safe fluent prose over wild bombast, and the documented weakness of LLM humor judgment vs humans — is invisible to no_self_judging, ensemble diversity, distractors, and null calibration alike. Demoting human calibration to an optional appendix means the headline metric is inter-LLM consensus taste on humor, the construct where LLM-human agreement is weakest, marketed as funniness. This is v1's judge-centrism with better statistics and worse epistemics.

WEAKNESSES:
- The headline metric is defined two incompatible ways in one document: section 1 gives five judged dimensions including culture (w = .294/.294/.176/.118/.118); section 2 folds culture into humor (w = .333/.333/.200/.133). Which weight vector is the benchmark? Also false 3-decimal precision on renormalized arbitrary priors.
- Folding CULTURE into source-blind HUMOR makes cultural adaptation unjudgeable: a judge cannot reward substitution of a reference it is forbidden from seeing. Culture is a relation to the source by the proposal's own taxonomy.
- Round-robin O(N^2) silently contradicts the cited JP-TL-Bench design (anchored comparison against a fixed reference output is O(N)); no justification is given for paying 5x for full round-robin at N=11, and N is hardcoded while leaderboards accrete systems — at N=25 the judging bill is ~$6k+/run under the corrected two-call cost model.
- Bootstrap cluster count is factually wrong: the corpus has 2 chains + 4 singletons = 6 clusters, not 7. Cluster bootstrap with 6 clusters does not just give 'wide CIs' — it gives unreliable coverage; the honest statement is that no defensible interval exists until the corpus expands.
- Corpus economics are fantasy: '~2 days of writing' for 40-50 timed, feature-quota'd, gag-chained segments of genuinely funny pastiche, recurring once per release forever, is the project's real cost — comedy authorship, not the $350 API bill. LLM-drafted seasons additionally introduce corpus-judge homophily (a season drafted by model family X is stylistically easier for family X and its judges).
- Threshold garden with no multiplicity control: halo Cramer's V > 0.15, position bias > 0.25, family delta > 5 points, cross-season contamination flags — several arbitrary tripwires, each triggering judge rotation or public flags, will fire by chance at this data volume, and each rotation churns the measurement instrument, breaking the cross-release comparability the anchor supposedly provides.
- Null calibration is underspecified: at temperature-0 generation, two 'independent' generations are identical texts, judges tie trivially, and the check passes vacuously. It only tests anything if generation sampling is nonzero — unstated.
- Temperature-0 judging is not determinism on modern MoE/batched serving stacks; reproducibility actually rests entirely on the judgment cache. Say so. Also eSpeak-NG version (which changes phoneme timing across releases) is absent from run.lock while being the eligibility oracle.
- The pre-registered headline hypothesis (DRB rankings diverge from FLORES-style rankings) is trivially confirmable: 6-cluster noise alone guarantees rank divergence. Needs a noise-null (rank correlation vs. bootstrap-expected correlation under H0), else the 'headline result' is unfalsifiable in the direction that matters.
- Whole-episode single-call translation: 45-segment structured output invites truncation and conflates long-output instruction-following with translation ability; retry policy unspecified (retries at temp>0 change outputs); a single generation per system means generation-sampling variance is never propagated into headline CIs — the null-calibration pseudo-pair estimates that noise but nothing consumes it.
- Injection pre-screen auto-forfeit on 'English meta-text in a non-English translation' will false-positive on legitimate retained English (pop-culture phrases, loanwords, code-switching for comic effect) and issues public forfeit flags on a small classifier's word.
- The judged NAMES dimension either duplicates Callback Integrity or requires each system's full prior-chain renderings in the judge prompt — unbudgeted tokens, and an asymmetric context burden for late-chain segments. Pick one: computed chain consistency (cheap) or judged chain consistency (expensive); running both unreconciled is waste.
- Callback Integrity's edit-similarity + lemmatization is brittle for ja/zh (katakana vs kanji renderings of the same epithet score as inconsistent; lemmatizers per language are an ops burden across 6 languages); the escalation micro-judge is specified but feeds neither the score nor validation — orphaned machinery.
- D-bloat 'distractor' validates a deterministic threshold in code — that is a unit test, not construct validation; counting it as part of the validation suite pads the appearance of rigor.

MUST SURVIVE:
- Callback Integrity (presence x consistency over gag_id chains, computed from cheap extraction) — the single best idea in the proposal and genuinely absent from existing MT benchmarks; extend it with a source-correspondence term (does the target chain anchor map to the source chain anchor) to close the untethered-generation hole.
- Performability as a physical measurement with On-Air Rate as a first-class separate leaderboard column, outside the aesthetic weighted average — but as a soft penalty band with a forfeit only for egregious overruns (>1.4-1.5x, outside the TTS error band), plus a dead-air penalty on the underrun side; keep speakability.py's heuristic as cross-check against the synthesis oracle.
- Pairwise judging with an explicit tie model (Davidson BT), dual presentation orders folded disagreement-to-tie, per-judge position-bias reporting, and forfeited/degenerate cells regularized with priors — the statistical core is sound machinery once cross-cell scale standardization is added.
- Gag-chain-clustered bootstrap and a tiered leaderboard (overlapping rank intervals shown as tiers, never bare ranks).
- Corpus expansion with per-feature quotas as the first-priority, highest-leverage investment; the generator-spec/season protocol and canary GUID for contamination management — with an honest authorship budget.
- Cutting BLEU/chrF++/COMET-22 entirely: reference-based surface metrics on reference-free paraphrase-mandatory text are noise-to-inverted and a misuse magnet; publishing raw translations lets anyone compute them.
- COMET-KIWI demoted to catastrophe floor (empty/wrong-language/refusal/hallucination detection), never in headline arithmetic.
- no_self_judging, pinned snapshot IDs in run.lock (extended to pin the eSpeak version), the content-addressed judgment cache, and publication of raw judgment JSONL per release.
- The distractor suite + null calibration as necessary-but-not-sufficient instrument unit tests, with discriminant (not just convergent) pass criteria — plus the missing D-untethered distractor as the single most important addition.
- Per-dimension source-visibility control as a concept (source-blind humor/voice vs source-aware names/culture/fidelity), re-engineered as a two-call structure with the cost model corrected, and culture kept source-aware.
- The weight-perturbation (Dirichlet) robustness audit in place of small-sample human-regression weight recalibration.
- A small, scheduled, explicitly non-blocking human calibration sample restored to the critical path for the humor dimension specifically — 50-100 bilingual-rated pairs before the first public leaderboard, not an appendix 'when it exists': it is the only defense against shared judge taste, the domain's best-documented failure mode.

VERDICT:
## Verdict: right instincts, broken load-bearing joints — do not implement as specified

The v3 proposal makes the correct macro-moves: it kills reference-based metrics that cannot work on paraphrase-mandatory text, it takes performability out of the aesthetic average, it replaces a blocking human study with runnable instrument unit tests, and Callback Integrity is a genuinely novel, cheap, correct-construct mechanism. The statistical vocabulary (Davidson ties, order folding, clustered bootstrap, tiers) is the right vocabulary. But four load-bearing specifications fail on contact, and one epistemic hole runs under the whole building.

**It cannot be built as written.** The single five-dimension judge call and the per-dimension source-visibility split are mutually exclusive — one call cannot both show and hide the English. Everything downstream of that (the 5x cost cut, the $350 headline, the halo audit) is arithmetic on an impossible architecture. Corrected: two calls per comparison, ~$700 now, ~$2.4k at the expanded corpus. Still solo-runnable — but the proposal should have caught its own contradiction.

**It does not measure translation.** Trace every instrument: humor and voice never see the source; NAMES and Callback Integrity reward self-consistency, not derivation; FIDELITY checks the scene, not the English. A system that ignores the source and free-writes scene-conditioned bombast wins every column. The fix is cheap (a source-correspondence term in Callback Integrity, plus a D-untethered distractor that must lose), but its absence in a document this confident is alarming.

**The statistics overclaim.** Summing BT logits across separately fit cells combines numbers on different scales — effective weights are set by judge decisiveness, not the published w_d — and the open-weight anchor fixes the origin, not the scale, so cross-release comparability is asserted, not achieved. The 1.15x forfeit cliff sits inside eSpeak-NG's error band and its calibration procedure (synthesize the English) cannot calibrate target-language rates. And the corpus has 6 bootstrap clusters, not the claimed 7 — the proposal miscounts `/home/user/dear-reader-bench/data/segments.json` in the paragraph where it lectures v2 about statistical honesty.

**The epistemic hole:** pairwise judging fixes LitEval's scale instability, not its actual finding — misaligned judge *preference*, which survives head-to-head formats. Distractors catch flattened prose; they cannot detect a shared aesthetic prior across all judge families, and humor is the construct where LLM-human agreement is weakest. With humans demoted to an optional appendix, DRB-BT is inter-judge consensus taste with confidence intervals. A 50-100 pair bilingual-rated humor sample before the first public leaderboard is affordable, non-blocking, and non-negotiable.

**Path to salvage:** two-call visibility split; source-tethering term + D-untethered; per-cell logit standardization (or a joint model) before weighting; soft performability band with egregious-only forfeit and a dead-air penalty; regularized BT with guaranteed-eligible anchor; consider the cited O(N) anchored-comparison design before paying O(N^2); resolve the two contradictory weight vectors; budget corpus authorship honestly — it, not API spend, is the project's real cost. Keep everything in the must-survive list; the skeleton is worth the surgery.
