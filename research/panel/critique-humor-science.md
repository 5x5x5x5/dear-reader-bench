FATAL FLAWS:
- Underpowered headline at the shipped corpus size. JSR is a strict binary conjunction over ~30 jokes clustered in 12 segments: one gate-flip moves the headline 3.3 points, cluster bootstrap with 12 clusters is known to undercover, the mixed-effects model has ~3 gag clusters for its (1|gag_id) term and will not converge, and per-mechanism breakdowns put 2-3 jokes per cell. Every inferential claim in §3 and every nomological prediction in §5 silently presupposes the 150–200-joke corpus that does not exist. At launch the leaderboard is either all-ties or noise dressed as ranking — and the proposal buries corpus expansion as a roadmap item when it is a prerequisite.
- Gate F measures joke recognizability, not joke survival. A probe primed with 'this is the transcript of a comedic performance — mark where the audience laughs' will mark intended-laugh loci in dead, literal translations, because LLMs recover authorial intent through translationese effortlessly and can 'explain' the intended joke of a flat rendering (humor comprehension ≠ humor appreciation — a distinction the humor literature the proposal invokes actually makes). The load-bearing 'behavioral' gate therefore passes dead jokes; hindsight coherence is answered with LLM charity; survival then rests almost entirely on the unvalidated predictability contrast. No evidence is offered against this, and the lesion battery's caricature lesions won't surface it.
- The design punishes compensation, the strategy its own cited literature calls best practice. Locus-pinned survival (±15% performed-time match) plus Spurious Joke Rate jointly double-charge a translator who moves a non-portable joke elsewhere in the segment or compensates pun→zero here with zero→pun there (Delabastita's sanctioned strategies, standard in AVT/dubbing): the carded joke dies on locus miss AND the compensating joke inflates SJR. A benchmark on which the field's best human practitioners would score badly is measuring the wrong construct at exactly the point it claims literature warrant.
- Gate I, the flagship novel instrument, is cross-language and cross-mechanism incoherent as specified. Multilingual embedding cosine scales are language-dependent (anisotropy varies), so a fixed +0.10 margin against an English-source baseline is uncalibrated; the English baseline conflates model competence in English with joke structure (punchlines look more predictable in high-resource languages, so the gate gets easier in low-resource ones); and CALLBACK and rule-of-three ANTICLIMAX jokes are definitionally predictable — the Wheel's whole function is that you see it coming — so the gate fails or must be waived for a third of the mechanism taxonomy, and the proposal never says which. Also, sampled-continuation embedding similarity is a Rube Goldberg surrogate for LM surprisal, the direct, cheaper, literature-grounded measure.
- Goodhart surface: the gates are necessary-condition detectors whose conjunction is silently treated as sufficiency, and every fixed threshold is published. 'Gate-shaped unfunny text' — a surreal non-sequitur at each carded locus, final position, right length, gag tokens string-stable — passes F (recognizable as intended joke), I-a (unpredictable), I-b (LLM coherence charity), and M for all portable=False slots ('any legal mechanism passes'). Having deleted every preference instrument, the design retains nothing that can notice text is not actually funny. The anti-gaming section addresses stuffing, explanation, and length, but not this, which is the attack a mechanized benchmark invites.

WEAKNESSES:
- Threshold garden / researcher degrees of freedom: ~15 free parameters (±15% locus tolerance, cosine ≥0.6 with a 0.5–0.7 adjudication zone, +0.10 predictability margin, ≥0.8 isochrony, 2.0s laugh room, register |Δ|≥3, AUC ≥0.85 / specificity ≥0.8, κ<0.4, chrF ceiling, ±15% register-shift timing) all pulled from air. Calling them 'pre-registered' is theater when none was calibrated against anything.
- Single-annotator answer key: the maintainer writes the pastiche, writes the Joke Cards, and sets the thresholds. GTVH Knowledge-Resource annotation has documented modest inter-annotator agreement, and script-opposition labels are notoriously subjective. No second annotator is planned even on a sample, so 'checkable answers' means 'checkable against one person's guess'.
- locus_ratio false precision: performed-seconds loci are computed with speakability.py, a deliberately coarse estimator (self-described as good for flagging 20%+ overruns), then gated at ±15%. Estimator error can exceed the tolerance, especially in CJK where the char heuristics are crudest. Multi-joke segments (mog-003 has 2–3 laugh loci in 20s) make span-to-card matching ambiguous, and nothing specifies how ties are resolved.
- Cost and complexity understated. The lesion battery requires running the full probe pipeline on ~540 lesioned items, not just 'a few hundred generation calls'; Spurious Joke Rate requires probing all uncarded regions; the variant corpus doubles each leaderboard run. Dollars are still fine, but six probe modules + stats + lesions + variants is months of solo engineering wrapped around 12 segments — the machinery-to-data ratio is absurd.
- Lesion hand-verification '~1 hr/language' assumes the maintainer reads every target language. A solo maintainer does not read Japanese, Korean, and Yoruba lesion outputs; the verification step is unrunnable as described.
- Register instrument is the weakest link: a 1–7 lexical-grandeur scale is cross-linguistically naive (Japanese register lives in keigo and verb morphology, not vocabulary altitude); tagging by one cheap model ×3 reps violates the design's own cross-family ensemble rule; range-based Flattening Index is dominated by a single outlier clause; DTW will align any two trajectories.
- Probe ensemble spec bug: found = majority of 3, but the leave-one-probe-out sensitivity analysis leaves 2 probes with no tie-break defined. Small, but it means the advertised robustness check is not actually specified.
- COMET-KIWI as the sole fidelity tether is weak: it is WMT-trained and unreliable on creative paraphrase, and the rewrite baseline is handed segment.scene, so scene-consistent original comedy plausibly passes the floor. JSR itself contains no source-meaning term — a same-mechanism, different-joke translation scores 100% (script-opposition cosine ≥0.6 is a loose tether). The known-groups clause 'rewrite must be killed by the floor' is a self-destruct switch, not a defense: if KIWI can't separate adaptation from translation, the run is just declared invalid.
- Discarding anchored pairwise entirely contradicts the strongest documented protocol in the cited literature (JP-TL-Bench shows anchored pairwise stabilizes literary judging). v3 replaces one instrument with a documented, bounded bias by six probe instruments with zero literature on their bias modes, and asserts superiority instead of testing it. The claim that detection/explanation tasks are immune to LitEval-style bias is unsupported — instruction-primed detection has its own compliance/acquiescence biases — and several probes (hindsight coherence, register tags, translationese) have no pre-registered answer at all, despite the 'checkable answers' rhetoric.
- Lesion battery circularity: LLM-generated caricature lesions (explain-the-joke, flatten-register) are far easier to detect than the naturally occurring mild versions in real MT output; AUC ≥0.85 on synthetic lesions is weak warrant for validity on natural translations. Worth doing, but treated as sufficient license to publish a leaderboard.
- Reproducibility: temperature-1.0 n=8 sampling, probe ensembles, and provider model drift interact badly with strict binary gates — small probe drift flips jokes discretely, so JSR moves in 3-point quanta over time. A graded score degrades gracefully; strict conjunction does not. Pinned probe models will be deprecated by providers within the benchmark's lifetime.
- Spurious Joke Rate inherits probe over-detection: if the Cold-Open probe over-fires (likely, per the priming problem), every system posts high SJR and the anti-stuffing tripwire discriminates nothing.
- Binary-at-the-joke-level 'on purpose' discards discriminative information: dichotomizing graded evidence costs large statistical efficiency (classic psychometric result), which the design can least afford at n=30. 'Nobody half-laughs' is rhetoric, not a measurement argument — partial joke survival (chuckle vs. laugh) is real and the graded sub-scores it demotes to diagnostics are exactly what the tiny corpus needs for power.

MUST SURVIVE:
- Joke Cards: mechanism taxonomy, setup/punchline spans, portability flag, script-opposition labels, gag links. Cheap, one-time, theory-grounded (GTVH), and the substrate every good instrument needs — but add a second annotator on at least a sample.
- Source-blind probing as the default posture, and especially the detection-asymmetry assay (probe with vs. without the English source; explicable-only-with-source = certified translationese). This is the best single idea in the proposal — a behavioral operationalization of the rubric's 'survives only via back-translation' clause.
- Seeded surface-variant corpora keyed to mechanism-level cards as the contamination defense — the observation that mechanism-level ground truth makes cheap variant generation possible is genuinely clever and should anchor the contamination story.
- Delivery suite extensions to speakability.py: punchline placement in performed seconds and laugh-room after the final punchline — cheap, computed, domain-true, and genuinely absent from all MT evaluation. Soften and calibrate the tolerances against the estimator's actual error.
- Gag Ledger: deterministic cross-segment consistency checks on gag renderings and callback integrity — nearly free, catches a document-level failure invisible to every sentence-level metric.
- The autopsy / cause-of-death taxonomy (died-by-literalism / explanation / flattening / timing / inconsistency) as the diagnostic layer replacing MQM categories.
- The lesion battery as the non-blocking validation harness — demoted to necessary-but-not-sufficient warrant, with honest accounting of its full probe-pipeline cost and of which languages the maintainer can actually verify.
- Known-groups anchors in every run: forced-literal baseline, frontier system, unconstrained-rewrite baseline, with required orderings and loud failure.
- No-self-probing, cross-provider probe families, and leave-one-probe-out sensitivity reporting (with the tie-break spec fixed).
- The scoping position: within-language rankings are the product; cross-language comparisons ship labeled diagnostic-only (probe-competence confound).
- The exclusions: BLEU out entirely, direct funniness ratings out forever, COMET-22 out, chrF++ demoted to contamination tripwire, private track declared contaminated by default.
- Paired permutation testing on joke-level outcomes with sub-significance rankings printed as ties, and cluster-aware resampling — once the corpus is large enough to support them.
- Spurious Joke Rate as a reported concept — but redefined to be compensation-aware (a segment-level joke budget: count of comic loci per segment vs. source, rather than locus-pinned extras counted as spurious).

VERDICT:
## Verdict: right lens, over-mechanized instrument — the core ideas must survive, the headline as specified must not ship

The proposal's central move — replace preference judgments with behaviorally testable properties of jokes, keyed to a one-time mechanism-level annotation — is the correct response to LitEval-style judge bias and is the strongest thinking any of the three iterations has produced. Joke Cards, source-blind probing with the detection-asymmetry assay, the delivery suite, the gag ledger, the lesion battery, and the variant-corpus contamination defense should anchor any final design.

But the headline metric, as specified, is a validity-theater machine with five breaks:

1. **It cannot produce a ranking at the shipped corpus size.** Strict binary JSR over ~30 jokes in 12 segment clusters means 3.3-point quanta, undercovered bootstrap CIs, a mixed-effects model that will not converge, and per-mechanism cells of 2–3 jokes. The entire statistical and nomological apparatus is written for a 200-joke corpus that is listed as a roadmap item. Corpus expansion is a prerequisite, not an enhancement; alternatively, the launch headline must be graded, not strict — dichotomization throws away exactly the statistical efficiency a 30-joke corpus cannot spare.

2. **Gate F measures recognizability, not survival.** A probe told 'this is a comedic performance transcript' will mark the intended-laugh loci of dead literal translations, because LLMs recover intent through translationese effortlessly. Comprehension is not appreciation — a distinction the humor literature the proposal cites actually insists on. The 'behavioral' framing does not immunize detection tasks against judge pathology; it relocates it.

3. **It punishes compensation** — the pun-displacement strategy Delabastita and the AVT literature treat as best practice — by double-charging locus misses and Spurious Joke Rate. A benchmark on which the field's best human translators would lose is measuring the wrong construct.

4. **Gate I, the flagship, is uncalibrated across languages and incoherent across mechanisms.** Embedding-cosine margins are not comparable across languages; the English predictability baseline conflates model competence with joke structure; and callbacks are definitionally predictable — the Wheel gag fails the gate by design. LM surprisal with per-language normalization is the direct measure the sampling-plus-embedding contraption approximates badly.

5. **Fully mechanical published gates are a Goodhart target.** The gates test necessary conditions; the design treats their conjunction as sufficiency and has deleted every instrument that could notice gate-shaped unfunny text (surreal non-sequiturs at carded loci pass every gate for non-portable slots).

The literature posture is also selectively honest: it wields LitEval against pairwise judging while asserting, without evidence, that six novel zero-literature probe instruments are bias-free, and it discards anchored pairwise — the one protocol with *documented* stabilization (JP-TL-Bench) — instead of running it as a comparison arm. The honest design keeps v2's instinct in miniature: run the pairwise ensemble alongside the probe gates and report their agreement; where they diverge is either a probe failure or the paper's most interesting finding.

Solo-runnability is closer to the truth than v1/v2 but still oversold: the lesion battery costs a full probe-pipeline run over ~540 items, SJR requires probing uncarded regions, variants double each run, and lesion hand-verification assumes the maintainer reads every target language. Dollars are fine; the six-instrument, ~15-free-parameter engineering surface around 12 segments is not.

**What a final design should look like:** Joke Cards + source-blind probes + delivery suite + gag ledger + autopsies + lesion battery + known-groups anchors + variant corpora, with (a) a graded joke-level survival score at launch and strict JSR reserved for the expanded corpus; (b) Gate F rescored on the detection *asymmetry* (found blind vs. found-with-source) rather than blind detection alone; (c) segment-scoped, compensation-aware survival; (d) Gate I rebuilt on per-language-normalized surprisal with mechanism-conditional applicability (callbacks exempt); and (e) an anchored pairwise arm retained as a cross-check, not a headline. The proposal's theory is the right one. Its instrument, as written, would publish confident numbers about constructs it has not measured, on a corpus that cannot support them.
