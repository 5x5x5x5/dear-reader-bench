FATAL FLAWS:
- Source-blind judging over 85% of DRS weight (voice .30 + humor .30 + names .15 + culture .10) changes the measured construct from comedy TRANSLATION to scene-conditioned comedy GENERATION. The only source-anchored signal left in the headline is fidelity at 0.15, judged against a one-sentence scene summary ('The man touches the cheese wheel; dust rises'). The dominant strategy — and the natural behavior of drift-prone creative models even without deliberate gaming — is wholesale replacement: write original bombastic target-language comedy about the scene. Worse, RESEARCH.md pre-registers 'rankings will diverge from adequacy rankings' as the hoped-for headline result; source-blind judging manufactures that divergence by construction, making the benchmark's central hypothesis circular and unfalsifiable.
- The performability gate contradicts its own construct and is concretely gameable at the code level. (a) Zero DRS penalty up to 1.25x the window is a verbosity SAFE HARBOR, not a verbosity punishment — a rendition at 1.2x the window cannot be performed in the window (isometric-MT/dubbing tolerance is ~±10%), yet DRS ignores speakability.py's own continuous penalty that starts at 0.95. Padding to just under 1.25x estimated buys more jokes for free. (b) The cliff at 1.25 sits inside the estimator's own stated error band ('good enough to flag 20%+ overruns'), so near-threshold classification — which forfeits ALL five dimensions — is coin-flip noise, exactly the gate-amplifies-noise failure the proposal warns about and then waves off because the instrument is 'computed'. (c) estimate_seconds is orthographically exploitable: the Latin-script branch regex [^\W\d_]+ counts digits as zero syllables, acronyms as ~1 syllable ('USSR' = 1, spoken = 4), and the ja/zh/ko branches count ONLY kana/kanji/hanzi/hangul — romaji, Latin loanwords, and numerals embedded in CJK text contribute zero estimated duration. The only non-compensatory element of the benchmark can be defeated by orthography, and the §5.5 TTS calibration only refits rate constants, which does not fix zero-counting.
- phi_d = mean win probability vs. the field makes DRS roster-relative: add two weak systems and every DRS inflates; drop one and rankings' meaning shifts. Releases are not comparable, per-language DRS values are not comparable (different effective judge competence per language), and there are no anchor systems — while the proposal cites JP-TL-Bench, whose contribution is precisely anchoring against a FIXED baseline for cross-run stability. Compounded by 'frozen judges' being unachievable on hosted APIs (judge models retire within ~a year), the batch-release longitudinal story is fiction: release N's health panel can never be re-run, and no plan exists to archive raw judgments or pin an open-weight judge core.
- The Erho2 >= 0.80 release criterion smuggles a blocking human dependency back in as authorship. The D-study will demand ~40-60 segments — 3-5 more pastiche films of genuinely funny, blueprint-constrained (every Feature >= 8 segments, every gag arc >= 3 segments with payoff), human-written WPDR-grade comedy — per corpus rotation, forever. A solo maintainer either can't ship (the criterion blocks) or fills the corpus with LLM-written pastiche, which contaminates the construct: LLM-flavored source comedy, potential family-style advantage for the authoring model's relatives, and a benchmark ceiling capped at ecosystem comedy quality. And the D-study that sets the target is itself projected from variance components estimated on 12 segments, which are statistically worthless.
- The probe battery is oversold as 'the non-human replacement for v2's human meta-evaluation.' It is a floor/regression test, not a validity warrant: (a) extreme synthetic degradations (extreme-groups design) validate gross sensitivity, not discrimination in the operating range where frontier systems actually differ; (b) the battery contains NO probe for the one empirically documented judge failure mode — LitEval's directional preference for fluent MT-ese over inspired-but-rougher renditions. The proposal's claim that pairwise judging makes judge bias 'cancel by construction' confuses severity/leniency MAIN effects (which cancel) with preference-DIRECTION biases (which pairwise fully preserves); LitEval bias is the latter. (c) LLM-generated probes judged by LLM judges share the ecosystem's blind spots — the battery cannot detect what no model can perceive, and probe rewrites by one family may be detectable via stylistic artifacts, inflating sensitivity for shallow reasons.
- The names dimension violates the proposal's own stated principles twice: it mixes a field-relative win probability (phi, 60%) with an absolute edit-similarity score (40%) — the exact scale-incommensurability sin §1.2 says DRS exists to solve — and mean pairwise edit similarity punishes deliberate gag mutation, which §6's own construct definition ('an epithet must recur, MUTATE DELIBERATELY, and pay off') requires and which the source corpus itself exhibits (the pants gag mutates every appearance). The extraction task also stops being 'extraction, not judgment' the moment a system legitimately transposes a gag onto different lexical material.

WEAKNESSES:
- Judge-slate-per-pair confound: excluding both candidates' families means measurement error is correlated with system identity — pairs involving the strongest judge families are judged by systematically weaker/noisier slates, which in a Davidson fit compresses those systems' ability estimates. No analysis or mitigation is proposed; 'with 11 families this always has slack' addresses feasibility, not the confound.
- Cluster bootstrap over 12 segment clusters undercovers badly (rule of thumb: <20-30 clusters); the v3.0 rank ranges will be either misleadingly tight or uselessly wide, and the proposal ships them as the headline uncertainty story anyway.
- Krippendorff alpha floor of 0.35 is far below any conventional threshold (0.667 tentative); majority-of-3 on near-chance agreement means per-item verdicts barely beat coin flips, pushing all reliability onto segment count — which circles back to the corpus-size problem.
- Order-disagreement-to-tie plus forfeit-to-tie pollutes the Davidson tie parameter: it conflates 'genuinely equivalent renditions' with 'judge confused' and 'both unperformable', three different constructs feeding one parameter.
- Payoff lift is confounded: context helps for non-gag reasons (voice continuity, name familiarity), so lift is not a pure gag-payoff measure; as a difference score its reliability will be low (classic Lord/Cronbach-Furby result when components correlate); and if ever folded into DRS it incentivizes payoffs deliberately opaque standalone.
- Cost is undersold: at 60 segments the full design is ~119k composite judge calls per release (55 pairs x 60 segments x 3 judges x 2 orders x 6 languages) before probes, 5% retest, 5% halo subsample, double extraction, and payoff-lift doubling — roughly $1-5k and ~150-200M tokens per release with frontier judges. Runnable, but 'still one-person-runnable' deserves the arithmetic it doesn't show.
- Pre-registered rules will fire on noise at current n: the culture-collapse rule (r > 0.9 over 11 systems has a huge CI), the 80%-sensitivity probe bar (binomial CI of ±20+ points at 12 segments), and the judge-exclusion thresholds all make binding decisions from hopelessly small samples.
- No language-identification or code-switching check: combined with source-blind judging and the CJK duration exploit, a Japanese rendition salted with romaji English is simultaneously duration-free under the estimator and potentially judge-rewarded — a cheap computed lang-ID gate is missing.
- Mid-release judge exclusion is operationally unspecified: if a judge falls below the 60% position-consistency floor after data collection, is the release recomputed with 2 judges, re-run with a replacement, or shipped flagged? Pre-registration freezes the slate; the exclusion rule unfreezes it; the conflict is unresolved.
- Reproducibility-vs-contamination tension unaddressed: publishing system outputs (needed for auditability of a judge-based benchmark) puts target-language solutions into the next pretraining cycle, defeating the 'no target-side solutions exist' contamination argument.
- 'Published translations of WPDR essentially don't exist' is asserted, not verified — fan-sub communities are exactly where such translations would live, and the private-track cleanliness claim rests on it.
- The genre brief in the source-blind prompt is a mono-style oracle: it specifies one flavor of bombast, which both narrows the construct (WPDR's register whiplash includes deadpan flatness the brief may penalize) and hands systems a style spec to stuff — the hollow-bombast probe checks gross stuffing but not calibrated stuffing.
- The 'only computed constructs may gate' slogan launders 'computed' as 'valid': speakability is fully specified but not yet validated (TTS calibration is future work) and is the single most gameable instrument in the design; the principle should be 'only VALIDATED instruments may gate.'
- The halo quantification (5% isolated-call subsample) measures common-method variance but the design does nothing with the number — no correction, no threshold, no consequence is pre-registered.

MUST SURVIVE:
- speakability.py as a computed instrument with the §5.5 TTS criterion study (cheap, real criterion validity; a genuinely good idea) — but feeding a continuous penalty in DRS, not a cliff gate, and with the orthography holes (digits, acronyms, Latin-in-CJK) fixed and included in the TTS refit.
- Pairwise comparative judging with both presentation orders always run, position-swap consistency as a free per-judge reliability statistic, and narrow per-dimension questions instead of one holistic verdict.
- BT-family aggregation with cluster-bootstrap uncertainty, rank ranges instead of point ranks, and the refusal to declare separations the data cannot support — augmented with fixed anchor systems/translations for cross-release comparability.
- PREREGISTRATION.md: hashed, frozen prompts/weights/thresholds per release, batch releases only, any change bumps the major version — plus the cut of live/drifting Elo leaderboards.
- The probe battery — reframed as a necessary-but-not-sufficient automated regression test for judge sanity, run whenever judges rotate, with an added fluency-bias probe class targeting the LitEval failure mode.
- Payoff lift (§6b): the within-system, within-judge context-vs-no-context contrast is the genuinely novel mechanism no MT benchmark has and the closest thing to an experiment in the design; keep as a reported column with its reliability measured before it touches the headline.
- The formative-construct stance: no cross-dimension Cronbach's alpha, per-component validity cases, and the Dirichlet weight-sensitivity analysis replacing the underpowered human-preference regression (cut #5 is correct and important).
- Instrument verdicts: BLEU excluded from the headline, chrF++ reframed as a pre-registered discriminant-validity witness with a published null/negative prediction, COMET-KIWI as diagnostic/anomaly-detector only, MQM spans cut (the adequacy-ontology argument is sound).
- Humans as a pre-registered, non-blocking upgrade path with a frozen slot-in protocol — the correct resolution of the v1/v2 tension.
- No-self-judging, the judge-deviation heatmap, canary GUID, corpus versioning, and the document-level frozen-harness translation protocol (no per-system prompt tuning, no glossary side-channel).
- The G-study/D-study framing as an honest way to answer 'how big must the corpus be' — with the caveat that its variance components are not trustworthy until the corpus is already bigger.
- The DIAGNOSIS behind source-blind judging (the audience never hears the English; source-visible judges anchor on source solutions) — as a judging CONDITION or arm alongside source-aware judging, never as the sole basis of 85% of the headline weight.

VERDICT:
## Adversarial review: dear-reader-bench v3 (psychometric design)

**Verdict: reject in current form; salvage roughly half.** The proposal's psychometric machinery (reliability panel, probes, pre-registration, formative-construct stance) is the best thinking in any of the three iterations, and several cuts are exactly right. But the two central design decisions — source-blind judging of 85% of the headline weight, and a cliff gate at 1.25x on a gameable estimator — respectively measure the wrong construct and hand attackers the benchmark's only hard constraint. A third structural choice (roster-relative phi with no anchors and un-freezable hosted judges) makes releases incomparable. And the release criterion quietly reintroduces the blocking human dependency the whole design exists to avoid, in the form of a comedy-writing corpus requirement.

### Fatal flaw 1: source-blind judging measures comedy generation, not translation

Voice (.30) + humor (.30) + names (.15) + culture (.10) = **85% of DRS is judged with no access to the source**. The only source-anchored headline signal is fidelity at 0.15, judged against a one-sentence scene summary (`schema.py`: `scene` is a single line like "The man touches the cheese wheel; dust rises"). Nothing with material weight ties the output to the source *performance*. The winning strategy — and, absent gaming, simply the natural behavior of drift-prone creative models — is to write original bombastic target-language comedy about the scene description. That is a different construct with a different name.

The proposal anticipates only "omission" and accepts it. The real failure is **wholesale replacement**, which fidelity-vs-scene cannot catch because the scene summary is compatible with infinitely many unrelated funny narrations. The offline "adaptation audit" doesn't defend the construct because it doesn't touch rankings.

This flaw is also self-serving: RESEARCH.md §4 pre-registers the hoped-for headline result ("rankings will diverge from general multilingual/adequacy rankings"). Source-blind judging **manufactures** that divergence by instrument design. The benchmark's central hypothesis becomes circular.

The *diagnosis* is right — the audience never hears the English, and source-visible judges anchor on source solutions (a real, documented bias direction). The *treatment* is wrong. Fix: judge both conditions (source-blind and source-aware) on a design that lets you estimate the source-anchoring effect, or add a weighted, source-aware adaptation-correspondence check (does each source comedic *move* — not its words — have a counterpart?) that is more than 0.15 of the composite.

### Fatal flaw 2: the gate contradicts the construct and is orthographically gameable

Three independent problems, each sufficient:

1. **The safe harbor.** Zero DRS penalty up to 1.25x the window contradicts §1.1's own non-compensatory argument. A rendition at 1.2x *cannot be performed in the window* — the isometric-MT literature the proposal leans on uses ~±10% as the isometric band. Meanwhile `speakability.py` has a perfectly good continuous penalty starting at 0.95 that DRS ignores. Result: padding to 1.24x estimated is free, and more text means more jokes and more judged wins. The claim that "the gate structurally punishes padding" is backwards — the gate *shelters* padding up to 125%.
2. **Cliff on a coarse estimator.** The estimator self-describes as "good enough to flag 20%+ overruns" — its error is the size of the threshold. Near 1.25, gate classification is noise, and misclassification forfeits **all five dimensions** for the segment. This is precisely the "a gate amplifies whatever noise its instrument carries" failure the proposal cites to justify gating only computed instruments. Computed ≠ accurate.
3. **Concrete exploits in `estimate_seconds`.** The Latin-script branch tokenizes with `[^\W\d_]+`: digits contribute **zero** syllables ("2000" free; "two thousand" costs 3), acronyms cost ~1 ("USSR" = 1 estimated, 4 spoken). The ja/zh/ko branches count *only* kana/kanji/hanzi/hangul: romaji, Latin loanwords, and numerals in CJK text are **duration-free**. A Japanese rendition salted with Latin-script material is simultaneously invisible to the gate and (with no language-ID check anywhere in the design) potentially judge-rewarded. The §5.5 TTS calibration refits *rate constants* only — it cannot fix zero-counting. Making this estimator the benchmark's **only** non-compensatory element maximizes the payoff for attacking it.

Fix: continuous performability penalty (the existing `performability_score` shape, threshold re-validated by TTS), a hard forfeit only at an extreme ratio (≥1.5) where estimator error can't flip it, unit-counting fixes plus a lang-ID/script-composition check, all covered by the TTS refit.

### Fatal flaw 3: DRS is roster-relative and releases are unreproducible

phi = mean P(beat rival) moves for *everyone* when the roster changes. Add two weak systems, all DRS inflate; the "operational sentence" is true only within one frozen roster. There are no anchor systems or anchor translations — while the proposal cites JP-TL-Bench, whose actual contribution is **anchoring against a fixed baseline** precisely for cross-run stability. Citing the paper and discarding its mechanism is a literature contradiction, not an homage.

Compounding: "frozen judges per release" is not achievable with hosted APIs. Judge models retire; release N's health panel can never be re-run; nothing in the plan archives raw judgments or pins an open-weight judge core. The batch-release comparability story is fiction as specified. Fix: 2–3 frozen anchor translations per language (including one deliberately mediocre one), report DRS against anchors as the stable scale; archive every raw judgment; include at least one open-weight pinned judge for a reproducibility core.

### Fatal flaw 4: the release criterion smuggles the blocking dependency back in

Erho2 ≥ 0.80 will demand ~40–60 segments — per the proposal's own projection, **3–5 more pastiche films** of genuinely funny, blueprint-constrained, human-written WPDR-grade comedy, refreshed at every corpus rotation. Who writes them? A solo maintainer either can't ship (the criterion blocks, which is v2's sin with a new face) or fills the corpus with LLM-written pastiche — contaminating the construct with LLM-flavored source comedy, plausibly advantaging the authoring model's family, and capping the benchmark at ecosystem comedy quality. Meanwhile the D-study that sets the target is projected from variance components estimated on 12 segments, which are statistically worthless. The corpus-authorship budget must be a first-class design constraint, not a footnote consequence of a reliability coefficient.

### Fatal flaw 5: the probe battery is a floor check sold as a validity warrant

Known-groups validation with **extreme synthetic groups** is the textbook way to overestimate an instrument's sensitivity in its operating range — detecting word-for-word puns vs. their unedited parent says little about discriminating two frontier systems' humor. Worse, the battery omits the one *empirically documented* failure mode: LitEval's finding that LLM judges prefer fluent MT-ese over professional human literary translation. The proposal's defense — "pairwise makes judge severity/leniency cancel by construction" — confuses rater **main effects** (which cancel) with **preference-direction biases** (which pairwise fully preserves). LitEval bias is directional; it survives pairwise intact, and no probe tests for it. Finally, LLM-generated probes judged by LLM judges share blind spots, and rewrite artifacts may make probes detectable for shallow stylistic reasons, inflating the reported sensitivity matrix. Keep the battery — it is an excellent automated *regression test* for judge rotation — but demote its epistemic role and add a fluency-bias probe class (polished-but-flat vs. inspired-but-rougher pairs, adversarially generated and manually spot-checked).

### Fatal flaw 6: the names dimension contradicts the proposal twice

Mixing field-relative phi (60%) with absolute edit-similarity (40%) commits the exact scale-incommensurability sin §1.2 claims DRS exists to solve. And mean pairwise edit similarity **punishes deliberate gag mutation**, which §6's own construct definition requires ("recur, *mutate deliberately*, and pay off") and which the source corpus exhibits (the pants gag never repeats verbatim: "whose pants are not" → "mighty pants surrender at the knee" → "pantsless and radiant"). Repairable — score consistency of the gag's *referent kernel*, report the ledger as its own column rather than blending scales — but as written the novel mechanism's computed half measures anti-comedy.

### Selected additional weaknesses (full list in structured output)

- **Judge-slate-per-pair confound**: excluding both candidates' families makes measurement error correlated with system identity — pairs involving the best judge families get systematically weaker slates. Unanalyzed.
- **12-cluster bootstrap** undercovers; v3.0's rank ranges will be untrustworthy either direction.
- **alpha ≥ 0.35 floor** is below every convention; majority-of-3 at that agreement barely beats coin flips per item.
- **Payoff lift** is confounded (context helps via voice continuity and name familiarity, not only gag payoff) and is a difference score, with the low reliability that classically implies. Right call keeping it out of DRS v3.0.
- **Pre-registered rules that fire on noise**: collapse rule on r over 11 systems; 80% probe bar on ~12 binomial trials.
- **Cost is undersold**: ~119k composite judge calls per release at 60 segments before probes/retest/halo/extraction — roughly $1–5k and 150–200M tokens with frontier judges. Runnable, but show the arithmetic.
- **Publishing system outputs** (required for auditing a judge-based benchmark) creates the target-side contamination the design claims cannot exist. Unaddressed.

### What must survive (any final design)

1. **speakability.py + TTS criterion study** — as a continuous penalty, with counting holes fixed. The cheapest real criterion validity in the design.
2. **Pairwise, both orders, position-consistency as free reliability data; narrow questions.**
3. **BT-family aggregation, cluster bootstrap, rank ranges** — plus anchors (see flaw 3).
4. **PREREGISTRATION.md, hashed, batch releases, no live Elo** (cut #7 is correct).
5. **The probe battery** as an automated judge-rotation regression test, with a fluency-bias probe added.
6. **Payoff lift** — the genuinely novel mechanism; nothing in WMT or the literary-MT literature measures cross-segment gag survival, and the within-system contrast design is sound.
7. **Formative stance + Dirichlet weight-sensitivity** replacing the underpowered human regression (cut #5 is the best single decision in the document).
8. **Instrument verdicts**: BLEU out, chrF++ as pre-registered discriminant witness, KIWI diagnostic-only, MQM cut.
9. **Humans as pre-registered non-blocking upgrade** — the correct synthesis of v1/v2.
10. **The source-blind *insight*** — as a measured condition or arm, never as the sole basis of 85% of the headline.

### Bottom line

v3 gets the measurement philosophy right and both keystone operational decisions wrong. Fix the construct anchor (source relation must carry real weight), replace the cliff with the continuous penalty the codebase already contains, add anchors and judgment archives for comparability, budget corpus authorship as the real scarce resource, and re-rank the probe battery from "validity warrant" to "regression test." What remains after those repairs is a benchmark worth building.
