FATAL FLAWS:
- G1's DEAD-AIR floor rejects the benchmark's own ground truth: running the repo's duration estimator over the 12 English source segments, 7 of 12 (mog-002 59%, mog-004 54%, mog-006 64%, mog-008 65%, mog-010 71%, mog-012 62%) fall below the proposed 0.75 speech-fill floor. The corpus windows encode pause-heavy delivery — the pauses ARE the comic timing ('The Wheel that hums. [beat] The Wheel that knows.') — so a gate that demands 75-110% TTS-measured speech fill flunks the source performance and forces every system to pad ~30% filler to pass. The flagship 'recording floor' gate measures speech density, not performance fit, and its own corpus refutes its band.
- The anti-gaming interlock's central claim is backwards: padding to cure DEAD-AIR does NOT trip VOICE-FLAT or JOKE-VOID. G6 asks judges to distinguish the candidate from a register-neutralized rewrite — generically bombastic filler makes the candidate MORE distinguishable from flat prose (longer, more exclamatory), so padding makes G6 easier to pass; and G5 only requires that A functioning joke exists somewhere in the segment, which padding elsewhere doesn't remove. Since G1 forces padding (see flaw 1) and no gate punishes it, the conjunctive design actively selects for bloated filler-stuffed translations — the exact degenerate behavior the proposal claims is structurally impossible.
- G2 beat sync scores synchronization to a film that does not exist. The public track is text-only pastiche — there is no shot of trousers tearing at 9.0s of mog-008; the 'spotted' timecodes would be invented by the corpus author against imaginary picture. On top of that, payoff onset is computed as syllable-share × TTS duration, assuming uniform speaking rate in a genre defined by non-uniform pause placement — error on the order of the ±2s tolerance itself — and `payoff_text` is self-declared by the evaluated system with no gate verifying the declared payoff IS the joke, so any system can nominate whichever substring satisfies the arithmetic. The flagship novel mechanism is a length-arithmetic puzzle wrapped in fabricated precision, not a measure of comedy sync skill.
- Binary conjunctive FPA on 12 segments has no statistical resolution: per (system, language) the headline is a mean of 12 correlated binaries (gag clusters reduce it to ~6 effective clusters), giving bootstrap CIs of ±30+ points; combined with the proposal's own 'report only CI-separated differences' rule, the leaderboard collapses into one undifferentiated tier — false equivalence presented as rigor. Conjunctive gating also multiplies per-gate noise into the headline (one flipped judge verdict on any of 7 gates flips the whole segment) instead of averaging it out, and pushes all scores toward a compressed floor where threshold micro-choices, not model skill, determine rank order.
- The judged gates abandon the two mitigations the project's own RESEARCH.md adopted from the literature and reintroduce the documented failure mode at the most load-bearing spot: G5/G7 are absolute single-text verdicts (LitEval's finding is precisely that absolute LLM judgment on literary text is miscalibrated; JP-TL-Bench's anchored-pairwise stabilization is discarded), and ALL human calibration is removed from the critical path. G5 is worst: 'is there a functioning joke' requires the judge ensemble to GET target-language humor — the known weakest, most correlated blind spot across providers — and a pun the ensemble misses becomes a hard JOKE-VOID fail rather than a diluted dimension score. Golden-defect tests cannot rescue this: separating seeds from synthetic joke_ablate variants is far easier than judging natural candidates, so ≥0.9 metamorphic accuracy is necessary but nowhere near sufficient, and on 12 matched pairs it can't statistically distinguish 0.9 from 0.7 anyway.
- The private track — a fixed constraint — is silently killed: every gate requires metadata the user-supplied transcript lacks (beats, scene descriptions, feature tags, gag_ids, a spotted timeline for ~170 minutes of film). Either the maintainer ships hand-authored spotting/scene metadata for the entire real film (weeks of labor, legally gray, never costed in the proposal) or the private track cannot run under v3 at all. The proposal never mentions the problem.
- Per-release definitional drift makes rankings incomparable and the maintenance load unrunnable solo: the α ≥ 0.4 floor (itself far below Krippendorff's own ≥0.667 minimum for tentative conclusions) demotes gates release-by-release, so 'FPA 61%' means different gate sets in different releases; the contamination plan requires authoring a NEW WPDR-quality comedy arc, spotting its fictional beats, hand-auditing new golden defects, and re-running the reliability battery every release; and locked API TTS voices version silently server-side, so G1/G2 verdicts drift with no translation changing. API cost is fine — the unrunnable part is per-release maintainer labor and instrument non-stationarity, and fixing the n=12 power problem (flaw 4) multiplies all of it by ~10.

WEAKNESSES:
- G6's discriminative power is a property of the uncontrolled neutralizer model, not the candidate: an under-flattened rewrite (one surviving epithet) makes everything pass; an over-paraphrased one lets judges pick on content or length cues (flattened bombast is systematically shorter — a pure length tell the proposal claims G6 is immune to). The gate degenerates to a near-tautology that only catastrophic flatness fails, adding conjunctive noise while measuring 'distinguishable from one specific rewrite,' not 'has the narrator's voice.'
- G3/G4's self-declared bible is vacuously satisfiable: declare the gag anchor to be the ordinary target-language word for 'wheel' and it recurs in every wheel segment by necessity; the claimed backstop (G5 mechanism audit) only checks that SOME joke exists in the segment, not that the callback is comedically operative, so a dead running gag passes both gates. The interlock is asserted, not designed.
- G3's fuzzy-match thresholds import a typology confound: token-set ratio ≥85 penalizes legitimate case-marked forms in agglutinative/heavily-inflected languages (Turkish, Finnish, Hungarian), and NFKC-substring for CJK forbids normal morphological variation — so per-language FPA differences partly measure script typology and matcher behavior, not skill, which then poisons both the macro-average and especially min-language FPA (an order statistic over confounded noisy estimates — the worst-behaved number in the design elevated to headline companion).
- The instruction-injection scan ('imperative judge-addressed patterns fail the segment for cause') is a false-positive machine on this corpus: the genre structurally consists of imperatives addressed to a listener ('Behold', 'Understand:', 'Dear listener!', 'friends, I must speak plainly'). Legitimate translations of the aside style will be failed for cause.
- The G5 mechanism taxonomy (pun/register crash/absurd epithet/adapted reference/carry-over/none) is closed-world: real mechanisms it omits (anticlimax, rule-of-three, comic simile — 'furniture escaping a house fire' is none of the listed types) force judges into either confabulating a listed mechanism (false pass) or answering 'none' (false JOKE-VOID). Taxonomy incompleteness becomes a scoring bug.
- No-self-judging plus an 11-provider registry means the judge ensemble composition differs per candidate system (each system is judged by a different 5-judge subset), so gate strictness varies systematically BY SYSTEM; binary conjunctive gating amplifies this unfairness that a compensatory average would have diluted.
- The director's-pick BT diagnostic is not small: O(systems²) pairs × subsample × languages × judges × 2 orders makes it one of the largest line items in the budget, and there is no protocol for what happens when it diverges from FPA — 'a flag to investigate' is not a decision rule for the only convergent-validity check in the design.
- COMET-KIWI as a 'departure gauge' publishes an unvalidated inverted-U hypothesis (strong systems should be moderately adequate) as a leaderboard exhibit, inviting systems to Goodhart toward a target adequacy band that has never been shown to correlate with adaptation quality.
- The 'target-side contamination is naturally near-zero' claim for the private track is asserted without evidence — WPDR has a 20-year fan community and fan translations/subtitles plausibly exist in major languages; the claim needs checking, not assuming.
- Golden-defect validation is one-sided: it measures whether gates catch constructed defects but never measures the false-rejection rate on legitimate stylistic diversity (seeds are one best-model output per segment), so a gate can pass the metamorphic battery while systematically rejecting valid creative solutions — the exact failure mode that matters for a transcreation benchmark.
- G7 (contradiction-only against 1-2 sentence scene descriptions) is so narrow it will almost never fire on real candidates — a dead gate that costs 5 judge calls per segment while its actual purpose (catching truncation) is only partially served.
- The [0.75, 1.10] band and the 'real tolerance bands' claim are presented as industry fact with no source; actual dubbing isochrony targets matching the original speech duration (not window fill), and VO/lectoring practice explicitly tolerates and uses silence — the proposal's AVT authority is rhetorical, not cited.

MUST SURVIVE:
- TTS-measured duration replacing the vowel-group regex — the diagnosis is verified and correct: the regex contains no Arabic, Hebrew, or Devanagari characters, so unvocalized-script words collapse to 1 syllable each and everything passes; duration must be measured, with cached published audio as an auditable artifact. But calibrate the band against the SOURCE performance's fill ratios (which the corpus shows run 54-89%), not an invented 75% floor.
- Underrun penalization in some form — the current speakability.py returning 1.0 for a 3-second take in an 18-second window is genuinely indefensible, as is the linear decay to zero at 2x window on the overrun side. The band values are wrong (see fatal flaw 1) but the shape of the fix is right.
- The full-script adaptation job with a bible/KNP deliverable — segment-isolated Translation objects genuinely cannot represent running-gag or name consistency; the evaluated unit must be script-level with declared term renderings. The self-declared bible needs an independent comedic-function check, but the deliverable shape and schema deltas (Bible, AdaptationResult) are the single best structural idea in the proposal.
- Golden-defect metamorphic tests (register_flatten, joke_ablate, knp_shuffle, pad, truncate, scene_swap) as instrument unit tests — the best available no-human validation mechanism, and it survives any scoring model; just don't mistake it for sufficiency evidence, and add a false-rejection arm on diverse legitimate translations.
- The retake-reason / defect-taxonomy reporting layer — domain-native, inspectable failure decomposition without MQM machinery is a real improvement over both v1 and v2, PROVIDED the codes feed diagnostics rather than a conjunctive headline.
- Detection-shaped judge questions (joke-presence audit, contradiction-only fidelity) as instruments ALONGSIDE anchored pairwise — the observation that 'is there a functioning joke here' is more tractable than 'which is funnier' is sound; the error was making it a solo hard gate instead of one graded signal.
- The flattening probe concept — register measured as signal detection against a meaning-matched neutralized self is genuinely novel and worth keeping as a graded diagnostic, with length-matched controls and a validated neutralizer, never as a binary gate.
- The payoff-placement construct — that the punchline must come where the source performance puts it is real and no MT benchmark has it; a defensible operationalization exists (ordinal check: source-final punchline must be target-final; position-in-text, not fabricated audio timecodes against imaginary picture).
- Cluster-aware resampling with gag groups as clusters — a correct statistical observation about correlated segments that any final design must keep regardless of scoring model.
- Cutting reference-based metrics (BLEU/chrF++/COMET-22) from the headline — the argument is sound: transcreation has no single reference and commissioning references in M languages breaks the solo-maintainer constraint; reference-free operation must survive.
- Canary GUID, corpus versioning, no-self-judging, both-order pairwise presentation, and per-release published reliability statistics (judge agreement, order consistency, self-consistency).
- The listenable target-language audio track as a leaderboard exhibit — cheap, auditable, and the most persuasive human-checkable artifact the benchmark can produce; keep it even though the gates built on it need rework.
- Feature-tag pass-rate breakdowns ('pun segments: 34%') — the corpus tags finally doing work is worth keeping in any aggregation scheme.
- The public/private FPA-divergence-as-contamination-diagnostic idea — comparing pastiche-track vs real-track performance as a memorization signal is clever and free.

VERDICT:
# Adversarial review: dear-reader-bench v3 "Recording-Floor Design"

## Verdict

The proposal's diagnosis of v1/v2 is largely right and several of its instruments deserve to survive. Its architecture does not. The conjunctive-gate FPA design fails on its own terms: **the flagship gate rejects the benchmark's own source material**, the anti-gaming interlock is backwards at its central example, the flagship novel mechanism scores sync against a film that does not exist, and the whole thing is statistically unresolvable on a 12-segment corpus. It also quietly kills the private track, which is a fixed constraint.

## The centerpiece: G1 flunks the English source

I ran the repo's own duration estimator (`speakability.py:estimate_seconds`) over the 12 English source segments in `/home/user/dear-reader-bench/data/segments.json` against the proposal's [0.75, 1.10] speech-fill band:

| segment | window | est. speech | fill |
|---|---|---|---|
| mog-002 | 14s | 8.2s | **59%** |
| mog-004 | 12s | 6.4s | **54%** |
| mog-006 | 16s | 10.2s | **64%** |
| mog-008 | 13s | 8.4s | **65%** |
| mog-010 | 19s | 13.6s | **71%** |
| mog-012 | 21s | 13.1s | **62%** |

**Seven of twelve source segments fail the proposed DEAD-AIR floor.** This is not a tuning issue. The corpus windows encode pause-heavy delivery because pauses are the comic mechanism of the genre ("The Wheel that hums. *[beat]* The Wheel that knows."). The proposal's own §0.3 correctly observes that punctuation pauses are "a large fraction of the clock" — then builds a gate that punishes translations for leaving room for them. A benchmark whose acceptance function rejects the ground-truth performance it was derived from is measuring the wrong construct: **speech density, not performance fit**. TTS measurement (the right instrument) makes this worse, not better, because TTS at fixed rate reproduces none of the performed pausing. And "real tolerance bands are roughly 75–110%" is asserted with no source; actual VO/lectoring practice explicitly uses silence, and dubbing isochrony targets the *original speech* duration, not window fill.

## The gaming interlock is backwards

The design's entire anti-gaming theory is "every cheap exploit trips a neighbor gate." Its central example — *pad filler to cure DEAD-AIR → JOKE-VOID/VOICE-FLAT* — is wrong in both directions:

- **G6 rewards padding.** The flattening probe asks judges to pick the bombastic text out of a candidate/neutralized-rewrite pair. Generic purple filler makes the candidate *longer and more exclamatory* — i.e., *more* distinguishable from flat prose. Padding makes G6 easier.
- **G5 ignores padding.** It asks whether *a* functioning joke exists at the humor-tagged point. Filler elsewhere in the segment doesn't remove the joke.

Combine with the G1 finding above: the band *forces* ~30% padding on half the corpus, and no gate punishes it. The conjunctive design doesn't resist the degenerate strategy — it **mandates** it. Similarly, the G3/G4 backstop fails: declare the gag anchor to be the plain target word for "wheel" and it recurs by necessity in every wheel-scene segment; G5's any-joke check never verifies the *callback* is comedically alive. The interlocks are asserted, not designed.

## G2 syncs to an imaginary film

The public corpus is text-only pastiche. There is no shot of trousers tearing at 9.0s of mog-008 — the spotting metadata would be authored against picture that doesn't exist. On top of that: payoff onset is projected by syllable share assuming uniform speaking rate (in a genre defined by non-uniform pausing — error on the order of the ±2s tolerance itself), and `payoff_text` is self-declared by the system under test, with no gate checking the declared payoff is actually the joke. Any system reading the published harness nominates whichever substring satisfies the arithmetic. The construct (payoff placement) is real and genuinely novel; this operationalization is fabricated precision measuring TTS artifacts and self-report.

## Statistics: a leaderboard of one tier

Binary all-gates-pass over 12 segments, with gag clusters reducing effective independent units to ~6, yields bootstrap CIs of ±30+ points on FPA. The proposal's own rule — publish only CI-separated differences — then collapses the leaderboard into a single blob: false equivalence dressed as rigor. Conjunctive gating also converts every noisy judge verdict into a headline bit (one flipped G5 vote flips the segment; a compensatory average would dilute it), and drives all scores toward a compressed floor where gate-threshold micro-choices determine rank order. **min-language FPA** — an order statistic over these noisy, typology-confounded per-language estimates (G3's fuzzy thresholds punish agglutinative morphology; TTS quality varies by language) — is the worst-behaved number in the design, promoted to headline companion.

## Against the literature it invokes

RESEARCH.md adopted anchored pairwise (JP-TL-Bench) precisely because absolute LLM scoring on literary text is unstable, and treated LitEval's judge-bias finding as the central design warning, mitigated by human calibration. v3 discards both: G5/G7 are **absolute single-text verdicts**, and human calibration leaves the critical path entirely. G5 is the worst case — "is there a functioning joke" requires the ensemble to *get* target-language humor, the most correlated blind spot across providers (majority vote does not fix correlated error), and a missed pun becomes a hard JOKE-VOID gate-fail instead of a diluted dimension score. The metamorphic battery cannot rescue this: detecting synthetic `joke_ablate` variants is far easier than judging natural candidates, and on 12 matched pairs, "≥0.9 accuracy" is 11/12 — statistically indistinguishable from 0.7. The α ≥ 0.4 reliability floor is itself below Krippendorff's own minimum for *tentative* conclusions (0.667), and the demote-per-release fallback means FPA's definition changes between releases — rankings that cannot be compared across time.

## The solo-maintainer accounting is wrong

API cost at 12 segments is fine. The unrunnable part is **labor and instrument non-stationarity**: a new WPDR-quality comedy arc authored per release (the hardest kind of writing there is, tossed off in one sentence), fictional beat-spotting per release, hand-audited golden defects per release, and locked "API voice" TTS that versions silently server-side so G1/G2 verdicts drift with no translation changing. Fixing the n=12 power problem requires ~10× corpus growth, multiplying all of it. And the **private track is silently killed**: every gate needs beats, scenes, tags, and gag_ids that a user-supplied WPDR transcript lacks; either the maintainer hand-spots ~170 minutes of someone else's film or the fixed-constraint track cannot run. The proposal never mentions it.

## What must survive

Despite the architecture failing, v3 contributes real assets: TTS-measured duration (the Arabic/Hebrew/Devanagari regex collapse is verified — the vowel class contains none of those scripts, so every word counts as 1 syllable); underrun penalization *in some form* (1.0 for a 3s take in an 18s window is indefensible — but calibrate the band on the source's actual 54–89% fill ratios); the script-level adaptation job with a bible deliverable (the single best structural idea — segment-isolated `Translation` genuinely cannot represent consistency); golden-defect metamorphic tests as instrument unit tests (the best no-human validation mechanism on offer, needing a false-rejection arm); the retake taxonomy as a *diagnostic* layer; detection-shaped judge questions *alongside* — not replacing — anchored pairwise; the flattening probe as a controlled, graded diagnostic; payoff placement as an ordinal position-in-text check rather than fabricated timecodes; gag-cluster resampling; reference-free operation; the listenable audio artifact; and public/private divergence as a contamination signal.

## Bottom line

Keep v3's instruments; reject its aggregation. The recording-floor *metaphor* — conjunctive acceptance, binary gates, no human anchor, pre-registered constants asserted as industry fact — is where every fatal flaw lives. A final design should run v3's measured/detection instruments as **graded signals feeding a compensatory score with hard failure floors only where the construct is fully specified and validated against the source performance** (overrun is; a 75% speech-fill floor demonstrably is not), keep anchored pairwise for the humor construct, and treat the bible deliverable and golden-defect battery as the permanent inheritance of this iteration.
